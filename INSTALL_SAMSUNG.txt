==================================================
 OneLife Sync — Quick install (Samsung phone)
==================================================

Your APK is built. To install it, you need to do two things on
this PC, then install on the phone.

----- ON THIS PC (one-time, ~30 seconds) -----

1. Open PowerShell AS ADMINISTRATOR:
   - Press the Windows key, type "PowerShell"
   - Right-click "Windows PowerShell"
   - Click "Run as administrator"
   - Click "Yes" on the UAC prompt

2. Paste this command and press Enter:

   New-NetFirewallRule -DisplayName 'OneLife 5000' -Direction Inbound -Protocol TCP -LocalPort 5000 -Action Allow -Profile Private

3. Close that admin PowerShell window.

----- ON YOUR PHONE -----

4. Connect the phone to the same Wi-Fi as this PC.

5. **Disable NordVPN on the phone** (Settings > Connections > More connection settings > VPN, or open the NordVPN app and tap Disconnect). NordVPN blocks LAN access from the phone.

6. Open Chrome (or any browser) on the phone.

7. Go to:

   http://10.212.16.44:5000/static/OneLifeSync.apk

8. The phone will download `OneLifeSync.apk` (~7.6 MB).

9. When the download finishes, tap it. Android will ask to
   "Allow this source" — enable it for the browser you're using
   (Chrome). Then tap Install.

10. Open the new "OneLife Sync" app from the app drawer.

----- IN THE APP -----

11. **OneLife URL** field, type:

    http://10.212.16.44:5000

12. **Bearer token** field, type:

    erqgNFvCS3Jin8dGXrZnqNP3E4wnE684CF8LbnQx0kU

13. Tap **Save**. Status should say *"Saved. Tap 'Open Health Connect' next."*

14. Tap **Open Health Connect**. Health Connect opens.

    In Health Connect, navigate to OneLife Sync's permission page.
    The path is usually one of these (try in order):

    - **Data sources** tab (bottom) → **Apps and devices** → **OneLife Sync** → toggle all 9 categories on
    - **Menu** (☰) → **Apps and devices** → **OneLife Sync** → toggle all 9 categories on
    - **Profile** icon → **Apps and devices** → **OneLife Sync** → toggle all 9 categories on

    If none of those work on your phone's Health Connect build, use
    the second button in the app instead:

15. **OR** tap **Open Android app settings** in the OneLife Sync app.
    This opens Settings → Apps → OneLife Sync → Permissions.
    Toggle on all the Health Connect categories shown there.

16. **Return to the OneLife Sync app.** When the app comes back
    to the foreground, it auto-checks permissions. The status
    text should now say *"Permissions granted."*

17. If it still says permissions are missing, look in Health
    Connect's settings for a section called "Data sources and
    access" or "Apps and devices" — OneLife Sync should be in
    that list. Toggle everything on.

18. Tap **Test connection**. Status should change to
    "Connection OK..." or similar.

16. Tap **Sync now**. The app will pull the last 24 h from Health
    Connect and post to your OneLife server. Open the OneLife
    dashboard on this PC — you should see new steps, heart rate,
    and sleep data.

17. Turn on **Background sync (every 15 min)** for ongoing sync.

----- ON YOUR PC DASHBOARD -----

Open http://127.0.0.1:5000 (or http://10.212.16.44:5000 from
the phone's browser) and check the Health page. You should see
data flowing in.

----- TROUBLESHOOTING -----

- "Connection refused" or "This site can't be reached":
    - Firewall rule didn't apply. Re-run step 2.
    - Phone not on same Wi-Fi.
    - Phone has NordVPN or another VPN on — disable it.

- "401 Invalid token" in app status:
    - Re-copy the token from step 12 — no extra spaces.

- "503 Ingest endpoint disabled" on server logs:
    - Server wasn't restarted with the token. Stop PID 31548 and
      rerun launch_onelife.py.

- Phone says "App not installed" / "Package appears to be invalid":
    - Re-download the APK. The browser may have partially
      downloaded.

- Forgot to grant a Health Connect permission:
    - Phone Settings > Apps > Health Connect > Apps and devices
      > OneLife Sync > toggle on what's missing.
